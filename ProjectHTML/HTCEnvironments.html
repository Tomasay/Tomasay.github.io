Overview

At HTC Creative Labs I was the technical artist on Viverse home environments, the space users land in when they put on the headset and return to between apps. Working alongside the team's 3D artist and designers, I owned the bridge between visual intent and what the hardware could actually deliver: lighting, shaders, VFX, and runtime performance.

Below is work from the two home environments I shipped, along with the social interactions I prototyped for them.



Viverse Home Environment - XR Elite


The XR Elite home had to hold a high visual bar on a standalone headset with a fixed frame budget. My role was taking the environment from art-approved to shippable without giving up the look the team had designed.

I reduced tri-count on incoming 3D models, consolidated materials into texture atlases to keep memory down, and set up static batching, LODs, and occlusion culling across the scene to cut draw calls. Where a visual target and the budget conflicted, I worked with the 3D artist to find the version that read the same in-headset at a fraction of the cost.





UX Shaders

I authored the environment's UX shaders, including the dynamic hands used for hand tracking. The hands render translucent white so they never occlude what the user is reaching for, with vertex colors driving a brightening falloff toward the fingertips. During interactions like pinching or pressing a button, the fingertips tint blue, confirming the input registered without needing a separate UI element.





Viverse Home Environment - Vive Focus Vision


The Focus Vision home shipped as part of the headset's OS, making it the first thing a user sees on startup. With more performance headroom than the XR Elite, the goal shifted from cutting cost to raising the visual ceiling.

Alongside the team's 3D artist, I drove lighting, shaders, VFX, and performance for the space, raising the runtime visual quality of the environment users see every time they put the headset on while keeping it inside its frame budget as the scene grew.





Home Interactions

I prototyped networked social interactions to give users something to do together in the home rather than just pass through it: physics-based basketball, a snow globe that swaps the skybox environment for everyone in the space, a group meditation area, and a shared space for watching movies and listening to music. Each was built to sync across users, so the home reads as a place to meet rather than a lobby.
